University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

M.S. Computer Science, 2017

Ph.D. Computer Science (Computer Vision), expected 2020

I am currently a PhD student in computer vision at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, advised by Professor Jan-Michael Frahm. I generally work in the realm of 3D computer vision. Previous projects have included analysis of neural-network-based salient feature matching algorithms for structure from motion and a post-processing AR system for visualizing landscape improvements. Most recently I have moved into deep learning, addressing problems of 3D semantics and geometry.

Outside of my research, I helped redesign and co-taught COMP776, a graduate level course in computer vision, with Dr. Frahm in the fall of 2017. Additionally, I was elected president of the Computer Science Graduate Student Association (CSSA) for the 2017-2018 academic year.

Boston University

M.S. Electrical Engineering (Signal Processing), 2015

I enrolled at Boston University two days after graduating from undergrad. I spent the first year in the Late Entry Accelerated Program (LEAP), a post-baccalaureate program designed to prepare non-engineering students for a graduate engineering degree. During this year, I was honored with a scholarship as a LEAP Merit Scholar.

Upon completing the LEAP program, I was admitted to Boston University's Masters program for electrical engineering, specializing in signal processing. In my final semester, I was a research assistant for Professor Venkatesh Saligrama at BU for projects related to person re-identification from video, visual search, and video retrieval.

Brandeis University

B.A. History with honors, Islamic & Middle Eastern Studies with honors, cum laude, 2012

Minors: Economics, Computer Science

At the beginning of college, I desired a career in law or government. Consequently, my undergraduate degrees from Brandeis University are in history and Islamic and Middle Eastern studies. In my senior year, I received honors in both majors for my senior thesis on the 1882 British invasion of Egypt.

I took my first computer science course as a junior, and became hooked on its analytical nature. I spent the last 3 semesters of college as a teaching assistance for the CS department and picked up a minor as well. Two days after graduating Brandeis, I started the transition to engineering through Boston University's Late Entry Accelerated Program (LEAP).
